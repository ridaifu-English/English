**The Tortoise and the Hare**

One bright morning in the forest, a Hare was boasting to other animals about how fast he could run.

Hare     : I am the fastest animal in this forest! No one can ( ① ) me in a race.
Tortoise : That's quite a claim, Hare. Are you really that confident?
Hare     : Of course! I can run circles around anyone here. Even you, slow Tortoise!
Tortoise : ( あ ) Well then, why don't we have a race? I challenge you to run to the old oak tree.
Hare     : You? Challenge me? This will be the easiest race I've ever won!

| A | All the forest animals gathered to watch the unusual race between the speedy Hare and the slow Tortoise. |

Fox      : Are you sure about this, Tortoise? The Hare is very fast.
Tortoise : I may be slow, but I have something the Hare doesn't have. ( ② ) Determination.
Rabbit   : Let's start the race! Ready, set, go!

| B | The race began, and the Hare quickly ran far ahead, leaving the Tortoise far behind. |

After running for a while, the Hare looked back and saw the Tortoise moving very slowly.

Hare     : ( い ) This is too easy! The Tortoise is so far behind. I have time for a little nap.
            I'll still win even if I sleep for an hour.

The Hare lay down under a tree and fell asleep. Meanwhile, the Tortoise kept moving forward slowly but steadily.

Tortoise : ( う ) Step by step, I will reach the goal. I won't give up.

| C | Hours passed, and the Tortoise walked past the sleeping Hare, getting closer and closer to the finish line. |

When the Hare woke up, he saw the Tortoise almost at the oak tree.

Hare     : Oh no! I must run as fast as I can!

But it was too late. The Tortoise reached the oak tree first and won the race.

Fox      : Congratulations, Tortoise! You won the race!
Tortoise : Thank you. This proves that ( ③ ) slow and steady wins the race.
