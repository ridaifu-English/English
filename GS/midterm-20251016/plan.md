## Implementation Ideas & Strategies

### Section 1: Listening Section (16 points - Knowledge)
- **Resource**: p77 (Nasca Lines), p91 (existing script)
- **Question types**: 
  - True/False statements about main ideas
  - Multiple choice comprehension questions
  - "What did they say about...?" direct recall questions
- Keep questions focused on factual information from the audio

### Section 2: Vocabulary Section (20 points - Knowledge)
- **Source**: Real Easy English conversation materials
- **Question formats**:
  - 5 Japanese-to-English matching (10 points)
  - 5 fill-in-the-blank sentences using vocabulary in context (10 points)
  - Example words from "Talking about Days Out": day trip, sightseeing, thrill seeker, adventurous, accommodation
  - Example words from "Talking about Games": board games, cheat, goody-goody, quiz, competitive
- Use the same vocabulary list that appears in the reading comprehension passage

### Section 3: Grammar/Structure Section (14 points - Knowledge)
- **Resource**: p71 (Miura-ori) and p85 (Nasca Lines)
- **Question types**:
  - Identify correct verb tense/form in context
  - Choose correct preposition/article
  - Sentence transformation (passive voice, reported speech)
  - Error identification
- Use sentences from the scripts as examples

### Section 4: Reading Comprehension (20 points - Mixed)
- **Resource**: Real Easy English conversations
- **Suggested topics**: "Talking about Days Out" or "Talking about Games"
- **Part A (Knowledge - 10 points)**: 
  - 5 multiple choice questions about facts in the conversation
  - Who said what? Where did they go? What did they do?
- **Part B (Critical Thinking - 10 points)**:
  - 2 inference questions (5 pts each)
  - "Why do you think...?" "What does this suggest about...?"
  - Require students to read between the lines

### Section 5: Writing Section (12 points - Critical Thinking)
- **Resource**: Real Easy English controversial topics
- **Question types** (3 questions × 4 points each):
  1. Opinion question: Express and justify your viewpoint with examples
  2. Analysis question: Analyze causes/effects or compare perspectives
  3. Discussion question: Present multiple viewpoints and reach a conclusion
- **Example topics**:
  - From "Talking about Games": "Is cheating ever acceptable in games? Discuss with reference to different situations."
  - From "Talking about Days Out": "Teenagers often stop wanting to go on family trips. Analyze the reasons and suggest solutions."
- Require extended responses with clear reasoning and supporting examples

### Section 6: AI Image Modification (6 points - Critical Thinking)
- Keep as planned with black & white image
