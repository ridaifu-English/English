# Australia Training Camp - Lesson Plan
## Student-Led Research & Collaborative Learning

**Target Audience:** 30 students preparing for English training camp in Australia (March)
**Duration:** 6-8 lessons (45-60 minutes each)
**Learning Model:** Student-led collaborative research and Kahoot creation
**Objective:** To research, curate, and share knowledge about Australian culture through group collaboration

---

## Overview: Student-Led Research Approach

### Why Student-Led Learning?
Instead of traditional teacher lectures, students will:
- **Research** their own topics in groups
- **Curate** important information collaboratively
- **Create** Kahoot quizzes to teach other groups
- **Learn** from each other through gamification

## Research & Creation Workflow

### Session 1: Introduction & Research (60 minutes)

**Minutes 1-10: Introduction**
- Teacher explains project
- Form groups randomly
- **Randomly assign topics to each group** (draw from hat or use random number generator)
- Distribute worksheet and materials
- Assign roles within groups

**Minutes 11-30: Initial Research**
- Research Leader guides team to reliable sources
- All members search for information
- Note-Taker records key findings
- Focus: Find 12-15 interesting facts about your topic

**Minutes 31-50: Curate Information**
- Discuss which facts are most important
- Decide what other students NEED to know
- Select 10 facts to turn into questions
- Organize information by importance

**Minutes 51-60: Begin Planning Table**
- Table Organizer starts filling in the planning table
- Group decides on first 5 questions
- Quality Checker reviews for clarity

### Session 2: Question Creation (60 minutes)

**Minutes 1-20: Complete Planning Table**
- Finish writing all 10 questions
- Write 4 answer choices for each (1 correct, 3 wrong)
- Note which images or visuals would help
- Quality Checker reviews each question

**Minutes 21-40: Create Kahoot**
- Kahoot Creator inputs questions into platform
- Research Leader finds images online
- Other members help proofread
- Test questions as you go

**Minutes 41-60: Review & Polish**
- Play through the Kahoot as a group
- Check for mistakes
- Adjust difficulty if needed
- Prepare 2-minute introduction for presentation day

### Session 3: Presentations & Learning (60 minutes)

**Format for Each Group (10 minutes):**
- **Minutes 1-2:** Brief introduction - "Why this topic matters for Australia"
- **Minutes 3-8:** All students play the Kahoot (5-7 minutes)
- **Minutes 9-10:** Quick debrief - Share the most important takeaway

**All 6 Groups Present:**
- Entire class learns from each other
- Everyone participates in all Kahoots
- Teacher can identify knowledge gaps
- Fun, competitive learning environment

## Kahoot Requirements & Quality Standards

### Minimum Requirements
Each group's Kahoot must have:
- **10 questions** exactly
- **4 answer choices** per question (1 correct, 3 incorrect)
- **At least 5 images** throughout the quiz
- **Mix of question types:** Easy, medium, and challenging
- **Varied formats:** True/false, multiple choice, identification
- **Time limits:** 20-30 seconds per question (adjust based on difficulty)

### Quality Standards
- Questions must test IMPORTANT knowledge for the trip
- Wrong answers should be plausible (not obviously fake)
- Images should help illustrate concepts
- Language should be clear and simple
- No spelling or grammar errors
- Questions should be interesting and engaging

## Assessment & Grading

### Group Project Rubric (100 points)

**Research & Content Quality (40 points)**
- Information is accurate and well-researched (15 pts)
- Sources are reliable and current (10 pts)
- Content is relevant and useful for the trip (15 pts)

**Kahoot Quality (30 points)**
- Meets minimum requirements (10 questions, 5+ images) (10 pts)
- Questions are clear, interesting, and well-written (10 pts)
- Difficulty is appropriate and varied (10 pts)

**Collaboration & Process (20 points)**
- All members participated in their roles (10 pts)
- Planning table completed thoroughly (5 pts)
- Group worked well together (5 pts)

**Presentation & Delivery (10 points)**
- Clear 2-minute introduction (5 pts)
- Professional delivery and enthusiasm (5 pts)

### Individual Participation (Bonus Points)
- Active participation in other groups' Kahoots (up to 10 bonus points)
- Top 3 scores across all Kahoots receive recognition

## Teacher Notes

### Facilitation Tips
- **Circulate during research:** Help groups find good sources
- **Check planning tables:** Review before they create Kahoots
- **Test Kahoots:** Play through each one before presentation day
- **Manage time:** Use timers to keep groups on track
- **Encourage collaboration:** Students should discuss and debate

### Common Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge:** Students choosing unreliable sources
- **Solution:** Provide list of approved websites, teach how to verify sources

**Challenge:** Questions too easy or too difficult
- **Solution:** Quality Checker role is crucial; teacher reviews planning tables

**Challenge:** Unequal participation
- **Solution:** Clear role assignments; rotate computer/paper tasks

**Challenge:** Running out of time
- **Solution:** Assign some research as homework; prioritize completing 10 questions

### Differentiation Strategies
- **Stronger students:** Can be Research Leaders or Quality Checkers
- **Visual learners:** Focus on finding images, creating visual questions
- **Students who need support:** Pair with helpful teammates, assign Note-Taker role
- **Advanced English:** Can help with question writing and proofreading
