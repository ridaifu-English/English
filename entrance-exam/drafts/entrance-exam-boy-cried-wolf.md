**The Boy Who Cried Wolf**

In a small village, there lived a young shepherd boy who took care of sheep on a hillside. Every day, he watched the sheep alone and felt very bored.

Boy     : ( ① ) I'm so bored watching these sheep all day. There's nothing exciting here.
Sheep   : Baa, baa...
Boy     : I know! I'll play a trick on the villagers. ( あ ) This will be fun!

The boy ran down to the village, shouting loudly.

Boy      : Help! Help! A wolf is attacking my sheep! Come quickly!

| A | The kind villagers immediately dropped what they were doing and ran up the hill to help the boy. |

Villager 1 : Where is the wolf? We came as fast as we could!
Boy        : ( い ) Oh... um... it ran away when it saw you coming. Thank you for coming!
Villager 2 : That's strange. We don't see any signs of a wolf.
Villager 1 : Well, as long as you and the sheep are safe. Call us if you need help again.

| B | The villagers returned to the village, confused but relieved that everyone was safe. |

A few days later, the boy was bored again and decided to play the same trick.

Boy      : Help! Wolf! Wolf! It's back! The wolf is here again!

| C | Once again, the worried villagers rushed up the hill, only to find the boy laughing and no wolf in sight. |

Villager 2 : This is the second time! Are you sure there was a wolf?
Boy        : ( う ) Yes, yes! It just ran away very quickly! Sorry for the trouble.
Villager 1 : ( ② ) This is very suspicious. I don't think he's telling the truth.
Villager 2 : Let's go back. But if this happens again, I won't come.

The villagers went home, feeling angry and tricked.

A week later, a real wolf appeared and started attacking the sheep.

Boy   : Help! Help! Wolf! Wolf! A real wolf is attacking my sheep!
       Please come quickly! This time it's real! ( ③ ) I'm not lying this time!

But the villagers heard his cries and ignored them.

Villager 1 : He's just playing another trick. Don't listen to him.
Villager 2 : I told you I wouldn't come if he cried wolf again.

The wolf killed several sheep, and the boy learned a painful lesson about lying.
