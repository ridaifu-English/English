# **Conjunctions Worksheet**

## **Page 1: Understanding Conjunctions (接続詞)**

### **What are Conjunctions? (接続詞とは？)**

Conjunctions are words that connect words, phrases, or sentences together. They help us join ideas and show relationships between different parts of our writing and speech.

接続詞は、単語、句、文を結びつける語です。アイデアを結び付け、文章や会話の異なる部分の関係を示すのに役立ちます。

### **Types of Conjunctions (接続詞の種類)**

#### **1. Coordinating Conjunctions (等位接続詞)**
同じ重要度の単語、句、または独立した節を結びつけます。

**FANBOYS:** For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So

- **And**: 情報を追加する
  - "I like pizza **and** pasta."
- **But**: 対比を示す
  - "I studied hard, **but** I still failed the test."
- **Or**: 選択を示す
  - "Would you like tea **or** coffee?"
- **So**: 結果を示す
  - "It was raining, **so** I took an umbrella."

#### **2. Subordinating Conjunctions (従属接続詞)**
独立した節と従属した節を結びつけます。

**よく使われるもの:**
- **Because**: 理由を示す
  - "I stayed home **because** I was sick."
- **Although/Though**: 対比を示す
  - "**Although** it was cold, we went swimming."
- **When**: 時を示す
  - "I'll call you **when** I arrive."
- **If**: 条件を示す
  - "**If** it rains, we'll stay inside."
- **Since**: 理由や時を示す
  - "**Since** you're here, let's start the meeting."
- **While**: 時や対比を示す
  - "I listened to music **while** studying."

#### **3. Correlative Conjunctions (相関接続詞)**
ペアで使用され、同等の要素を結びつけます。

- **Both...and**: 両方とも
  - "I like **both** chocolate **and** vanilla."
- **Either...or**: どちらか
  - "You can **either** walk **or** take the bus."
- **Neither...nor**: どちらも〜ない
  - "I have **neither** time **nor** money."
- **Not only...but also**: 〜だけでなく
  - "She is **not only** smart **but also** kind."

### **How to Use Conjunctions (接続詞の使い方)**

#### **Rules for Coordinating Conjunctions:**
1. Use a comma before the conjunction when connecting two independent clauses
   - "I wanted to go to the party, **but** I had to study."
2. No comma needed when connecting words or short phrases
   - "I bought apples **and** oranges."

#### **Rules for Subordinating Conjunctions:**
1. When the dependent clause comes first, use a comma
   - "**Because** it was late, I went home."
2. When the independent clause comes first, usually no comma
   - "I went home **because** it was late."
