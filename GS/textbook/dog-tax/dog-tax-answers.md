## Part 2: Listening Comprehension

1. What is today's topic?
   - **Unique taxes in the world**

2. Which two countries have a junk food tax?
   - **Taiwan and Romania**

3. Name two European countries that have a dog tax today.
   - **Any two of: Germany, the Netherlands, Switzerland**

4. Why did England introduce the first dog tax?
   - **England needed money to fight a war against France**

5. Does England still have a dog tax today?
   - **No, England doesn't have this tax anymore**

## Part 3: True or False

1. In Japan, people pay a tax when they use hot springs. **T**

2. Only Germany has a dog tax in Europe today. **F**
   - (Germany, the Netherlands, and Switzerland have dog taxes)

3. The first dog tax was introduced in France. **F**
   - (It was introduced in England)

4. England introduced the dog tax at the end of the 18th century. **T**

5. England needed money to fight a war, so they created many new taxes. **T**

## Part 4: Critical Thinking

**Open-ended question - Accept various reasonable answers**

Sample evaluation criteria:
- Student identifies a specific type of tax
- Provides clear reasoning for why the tax would be fair
- Explains how the tax would be effective in raising money
- Shows consideration for social/economic impacts

Sample strong answers might include:
- Luxury item taxes (designer goods, expensive cars)
- Environmental taxes (plastic bags, carbon emissions)
- Entertainment taxes (streaming services, gaming)
- With appropriate justification for fairness and effectiveness
