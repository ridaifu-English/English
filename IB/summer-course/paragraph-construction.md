# **Paragraph Construction Worksheet**

## **Page 1: Building Strong Body Paragraphs (本文段落の構成)**

### **What is a Body Paragraph? (本文段落とは？)**

A body paragraph is the main part of an essay that supports your thesis statement. Each body paragraph should focus on one main idea and provide evidence to support it.

本文段落は、論文の主要部分で、論文の主張を支える役割があります。各本文段落は一つの主要なアイデアに焦点を当て、それを支える証拠を提供する必要があります。

### **Basic Structure of a Body Paragraph (本文段落の基本構造)**

#### **T.E.E.L Method**
- **T**opic Sentence (トピック文) - Main idea of the paragraph
- **E**xplanation (説明) - Explain your point in detail
- **E**vidence (証拠) - Support with examples, facts, or quotes
- **L**ink (つなぎ) - Connect back to thesis or transition to next paragraph

### **Types of Supporting Evidence (支援証拠の種類)**

#### **1. Examples (具体例)**
具体的な事例を使って論点を説明します。

**Useful Phrases for Examples:**
- For example, ... (例えば...)
- For instance, ... (たとえば...)
- Such as ... (...のような)
- To illustrate this point, ... (この点を説明するために...)
- A good example of this is ... (この良い例は...)

**Example:**
> **Topic Sentence:** Technology has revolutionized how students learn.
> **Example:** For example, online learning platforms like Khan Academy allow students to study at their own pace and revisit difficult concepts as many times as needed.

#### **2. Facts and Statistics (事実と統計)**
客観的なデータや研究結果を使って論点を支えます。

**Useful Phrases for Facts:**
- According to research, ... (研究によると...)
- Statistics show that ... (統計によると...)
- Studies have proven that ... (研究により証明されている...)
- Research indicates that ... (研究は...を示している)
- Data reveals that ... (データは...を明らかにしている)

**Example:**
> **Topic Sentence:** Exercise has significant benefits for mental health.
> **Fact:** According to a study published in the Journal of Clinical Medicine, regular exercise can reduce symptoms of depression by up to 30%.

#### **3. Expert Opinions and Quotes (専門家の意見と引用)**
権威ある人物の意見や発言を引用します。

**Useful Phrases for Quotes:**
- As [expert name] states, ... (...氏が述べるように...)
- According to [expert], ... (...によると...)
- [Expert name] argues that ... (...氏は...と主張している)
- In the words of [expert], ... (...氏の言葉では...)

**Example:**
> **Topic Sentence:** Climate change requires immediate global action.
> **Expert Opinion:** As environmental scientist Dr. Jane Smith states, "We have less than a decade to make significant changes to avoid catastrophic climate impacts."

#### **4. Counter-examples and Contrasts (反例と対比)**
異なる視点や反対の例を示して、論点の複雑さを認識します。

**Useful Phrases for Counter-examples:**
- However, ... (しかし...)
- On the other hand, ... (一方で...)
- Despite this, ... (これにもかかわらず...)
- Although some argue that ... (一部の人は...と主張するが...)
- Critics point out that ... (批判者は...を指摘している)

**Example:**
> **Topic Sentence:** Social media has many benefits for teenagers.
> **Counter-example:** However, critics point out that excessive social media use can lead to anxiety and sleep problems among young people.

#### **5. Personal Experience (個人的経験)**
適切な場合に、個人的な体験を証拠として使用します。

**Useful Phrases for Personal Experience:**
- In my experience, ... (私の経験では...)
- I have personally observed that ... (私は個人的に...を観察した)
- From my own perspective, ... (私自身の視点から...)

**Example:**
> **Topic Sentence:** Learning a foreign language improves cognitive abilities.
> **Personal Experience:** In my experience learning Japanese, I noticed that my problem-solving skills improved as I had to think more flexibly about grammar and vocabulary.

### **Useful Transition Words (便利な接続語)**

**To add information:** Furthermore, Moreover, Additionally, Also, In addition
**To show contrast:** However, Nevertheless, On the contrary, In contrast, Despite this
**To give examples:** For example, For instance, Such as, To illustrate, Namely
**To show cause and effect:** Therefore, Consequently, As a result, Thus, Hence
**To conclude:** In conclusion, Finally, To summarize, Overall, In summary
