## Part 3: Listening Comprehension

1. She is Japan's 104th prime minister (or: first female prime minister / country's leader)
2. They are worried about: lower food prices, job security, and the growing number of old people (accept any two of these)
3. She worked as a TV anchor (or: she wrote books about politics / she played in a rock band)

---

## Part 4: True or False

1. **T** (She is the first female prime minister)
2. **F** (She is the 104th leader, not the 100th)
3. **F** (She played drums, not guitar)
4. **T** (She lived in the USA and wrote books about politics)
5. **T** (The article says women hope she will support same-sex marriage, and it mentions this as a goal)

---

## Part 5: Critical Thinking

**Open-ended question - answers will vary**

Sample answer points students might include:
- She has a difficult job because Japanese people have many concerns (food prices, job security, aging population)
- She has interesting experiences that could help her lead (rock band, lived in USA, TV anchor, wrote books)
- Students should reference her background and the challenges she faces
- Responses may discuss her support for gender equality as a positive or her position on other issues
